📉

COVID dip was real but partial recovery is underway

Language dropped from 66.7% (2017) → 62% (2021) amid pandemic school closures. By 2024 it recovered to 64% — still below pre-COVID levels. Math followed a similar path: ~65% → 57% → 60%.

📚

Language beats math at every cycle

Students consistently score higher in language than mathematics across all three rounds. The gap was ~9pp in 2017, widened to ~5pp in 2021, and settled at ~4pp in 2024 — suggesting targeted numeracy interventions are narrowing it.

🏫

State govt. schools on par with private schools

In 2024, state government schools matched private schools in Language (both 64%) and led in Mathematics (61% vs 60%). Central government schools performed lowest — a surprising reversal warranting further investigation.

👧

Girls outperform boys in language

Nationally, girls scored 65% vs boys' 63% in Language in 2024. Math scores were identical (60% each). Girls' language advantage has held across all three cycles — but they remain underrepresented in STEM pathways at higher grades.

⚠️

Caste-based hierarchy persists unchanged

Across all three cycles, the hierarchy Others → OBC → SC → ST is almost identically reproduced. Despite successive reforms, the rank order has not changed — structural inequity is not self-correcting.

🌾

Rural–urban gap widened post-COVID

In 2017, rural students performed comparably to urban peers in Grade 3. After the pandemic, rural students' scores fell more sharply in 2021. The 2024 recovery is uneven — rural areas lag behind, especially in math.
